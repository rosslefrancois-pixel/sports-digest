NHL · Stanley Cup
Cup Awarded · Yesterday
The Carolina Hurricanes won the 2026 Stanley Cup on Sunday, June 14, shutting out Vegas 3–0 in Game 6 to finish 16–3 in the postseason — the best playoff record since the 1987–88 Oilers.

Carolina Hurricanes are 2026 Stanley Cup champions — their second title, first since 2006, capping a 16–3 postseason run.
Jordan Staal wins Conn Smythe Trophy at age 37, becoming the oldest recipient in NHL history; posted a goal in each of the first five Final games.
Brandon Bussi stepped in for Frederik Andersen (pulled in Game 6) and made 22 saves in the clinching shutout in just his 43rd NHL appearance.
Goals from Taylor Hall (breakaway, 3:47 in), Jackson Blake and Nikolaj Ehlers powered the Game 6 win.
Carolina's championship parade is set for Saturday, June 20 at 11 a.m. in Downtown Raleigh.
