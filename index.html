2026 NHL Draft — June 26–27 at KeyBank Center in Buffalo; Boston holds the No. 23 overall pick and six additional selections (56th, 88th, 111th, 120th, 122nd, 206th).
The Bruins own five first-round picks across the next three drafts and have a decent amount of cap space heading into free agency (opens July 1).
Key free agents to watch: F Viktor Arvidsson & D Andrew Peeke — both UFAs if not re-signed; Sweeney said he will explore bringing either or both back.
Extensions already signed this offseason: forwards Lukas Reichel and Navrin Mutter are locked up.
Prospect buzz: James Hagens (2025 No. 7 pick) will not attend development camp — not injury-related, per insider Russ Macias.
The Athletic ranked Boston's prospect pool 19th in the NHL — Sweeney needs a strong draft to restock a pipeline thinned by trades.
Training camp opens Sept. 16 at Warrior Ice Arena — it will be coach Marco Sturm's second camp behind the bench.
NENew England Patriots
Minicamp Recap
The defending AFC champions wrapped three days of mandatory minicamp (June 9–11) with Drake Maye & new acquisition A.J. Brown generating immediate buzz; training camp opens July 24.

Drake Maye was the standout across all three minicamp sessions — particularly sharp during red-zone 11-on-11 work; Tom Curran already floated him as a potential No. 2 QB in the NFL in 2026.
A.J. Brown (traded from Philadelphia on June 1) caught two TD passes from Maye on the final day; safety Kevin Byard said the early connection will be a 'nightmare for defenses.'
Christian Gonzalez contract standoff remains the 'elephant in the room' — he attended minicamp but sat out competitive team drills; told reporters 'I want to be rewarded as a Patriot.'
2026 2nd-round pick Gabe Jacas (pass rusher) missed minicamp — had a procedure this offseason and has yet to sign his rookie deal; Vrabel: 'He had a procedure, and he's not under contract.'
Christian Barmore was described as 'locked-in' and even more disruptive than his 2025 campaign; DT Dre'Mont Jones ($36M FA signing) also earning rave reviews.
Season opens Sept. 9 in Seattle (Super Bowl LX rematch, Wednesday Night game); home opener vs. Pittsburgh on Sept. 20; international game vs. Detroit in Munich on Nov. 15.

Ohio State returns 8 starters on offense — four O-linemen, two WRs, and the entire starting backfield — giving Ryan Day elite continuity heading into 2026.
WR Jeremiah Smith heads an elite receiver room that also includes returnee Brandon Iniss and five-star freshman Chris Henry Jr.; CBS Sports projects both freshmen in prominent roles.

Training camp: Aug. 6–8 at Ohio Stadium; single-game tickets go on sale to the general public June 26. Halloween night USC game at the LA Coliseum is the marquee road date.
