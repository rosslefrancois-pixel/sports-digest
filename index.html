The Bruins hold the No. 23 overall pick in Friday's draft — a conditional first-rounder from Toronto that would have been top-6 had the Maple Leafs not won the lottery.
Boston has eight total picks this draft after the Gasseau/Sharks trade swapped a 4th (No. 120) for picks No. 104 & 157.
Extensions inked so far: Lukas Reichel (1 yr, $950K), Navrin Mutter (2-way, $850K), Simon Zajicek (2-way), and Attilio Biasca (through 2027-28).
Sweeney said talks with UFAs Viktor Arvidsson and Andrew Peeke are ongoing but no deal yet — "there's interest for both sides, just haven't found an agreeable term and AAV."
Puckpedia notes the Bruins want a true No. 1 center and a right-shot offensive defenseman; Elias Lindholm is among the most-explored players on the buyout calculator.
Development camp runs June 29–July 2; rookie camp starts Sept. 11; training camp opens Sept. 16 at Warrior Ice Arena.
